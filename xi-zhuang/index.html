布料市场的人

裁缝
cáifeng
tailor / seamstress
量体裁衣的手艺人
他找裁缝照着照片做了一件旗袍。
He had a tailor make a qipao based on a photo.
Cuts (裁) and sews (缝) — handles custom garments and alterations. Not the designer (设计师). Neutral tone on 缝 marks this as a noun. English "tailor-made" = customized does not map to 裁缝 — use 量身定做 for that.

老板娘
lǎobǎnniáng
proprietress — woman running the shop
管理店铺的女老板
老板娘，我的西装你们做好了吗？
Is my suit ready?
Not simply "female boss." 老板 is gender-neutral (any owner); 老板娘 is the casual address for the woman — often owner, wife, or both. The 娘 frames female business authority through a familial lens. Wrong register for corporate settings.

西装的世界

西装
xī zhuāng
Western-style suit
西式上衣与裤子的套装
这套西装挺合身的，颜色也很适合你。
This suit fits very well, and the colour suits you too.
The 西 marks cultural contrast — active in a closed modernisation-era set: 西装、西餐、西医. Not a general property of 西: 西瓜 and 东西 carry no Western/Chinese frame. Culturally coded as business formality and social mobility.

名牌
míng pái
designer label / famous brand
著名品牌；奢侈品牌
这件西装是名牌的，当然贵。
This suit is a designer label — of course it's expensive.
Prestige is definitional — 名牌 = luxury only. Not neutral 品牌 (any brand). Extension: 名牌大学 (top university). Polysemy trap: 名牌 also = "name badge" (别上名牌 = pin on your name tag) — entirely different register.

料子
liàozi
 liàozi Colloquial — implies tactile appraisal ("feel it, judge it"). The word a tailor naturally reaches for.口语，含上手感受、评判之意，裁缝自然会用的词。 有料子 = has real ability. 甩料子 = drop a hint. Both fixed; 布料 cannot substitute.有料子（有真本事）、甩料子（抛出线索），均为固定搭配，布料不可替换。

肩膀
jiān bǎng
shoulder
颈部两侧、手臂上方的关节部位
可是我觉得肩膀这里有点紧，袖子也有点长。
But I think the shoulders are a bit tight and the sleeves a bit long.
The joint and surrounding muscle. The shoulder seam must land exactly here for correct drape. In professional fitting speech, 肩宽 (shoulder width) is more idiomatic; 肩膀 stays on the body side of that distinction.
图片
袖子
xiùzi
sleeve
衣服覆盖手臂的部分，从肩部到袖口
袖子我可以改短，肩膀这里放大的话，我担心没有现在合身。
I can shorten the sleeves, but if I let out the shoulders it may not fit as well.
Fabric tube from shoulder seam to cuff. In compound types (长袖, 短袖) the 子 drops — don't say 长袖子. English "have something up your sleeve" (= hidden plan) has no Chinese sleeve-based equivalent.
图片
腰
yāo
waist (body zone)
身体中部；服装腰部区域
裤子长短正好，可是腰这里有点肥。
The trouser length is just right, but the waist is a bit baggy.
Felt zone ≠ measured number: 腰有点紧 (the zone feels tight) vs 腰围68厘米 (the measurement). 腰 also owns the lower-back pain sense (腰疼) — its range extends further posterior than English "waist."
图片
裤子
kùzi
trousers / pants
穿在腰部以下、覆盖两腿的服装
裤子长短正好，可是腰这里有点肥。
The trouser length is just right, but the waist is a bit baggy.
Counted with 条 (elongated object), not 双 (paired items) — 一条裤子. The 子 is frozen in the standalone noun; in compound types (牛仔裤, 短裤) bare 裤 is the root — don't append 子 there.

Interactive互动练习

合身程度

Four positions on the fit spectrum — two negative, two positive.合身程度的四个位置——两个负面，两个正面。

too tight ✗太紧 ✗ good fit ✓合身 ✓ roomy by design ✓宽松有型 ✓ badly baggy ✗虚肥 ✗

紧 jǐn

Too tight — constricts movement. Fix: 放大 or 改大 (let out).太紧——限制活动。解决：放大 或 改大。

肩膀那里有点儿紧，能帮我改大一点吗？
The shoulders are a bit tight — could you let them out?
⚠ 紧 also = urgent/tense (时间紧 = schedule is tight). English "tight" = stingy? That's 小气/抠门 — not 紧.

合身 héshēn

Just right — dimensional correspondence between garment and body. The goal of every alteration.正合适——服装与身体的精准对应，每次修改的目标。

裁缝把衣服改过之后，穿起来终于合身了。
After the tailor made the alterations, it finally fits properly.
合身 = dimensional fit only. English "fits you well" often implies flattering — that's 适合你/衬你. Intransitive: 你穿起来很合身 ✓ / *合身你 ✗.

宽松 kuān sōng

Roomy by design — intentional, stylish looseness. The tailor's intention, not their error.刻意宽松——有意为之的风格，是设计，不是失误。

她喜欢穿宽松的亚麻衬衫，夏天凉快又好看。
She likes wearing loose linen shirts — cool and stylish in summer.
Chinese draws a lexical line English blurs: 宽松 = tailor's intention, 肥 = tailor's error. Also: 宽松货币政策 (loose monetary policy) — same word, different domain.

肥 féi

Badly baggy — too much fabric, ill-fitting. Fix: 改小 or 收 (take in).虚肥——布料过多，不合身。解决：改小 或 收。

裤子的腰那里有点儿肥——能帮我改小一点吗？
The trouser waist is a bit baggy — can you take it in?
⚠ 肥 applied to people is blunt/rude (≈ unfiltered "fat"). Polite word for people: 胖. The tailor-shop sense is about fabric, not body.

肩膀 jiān bǎng

The shoulder joint and surrounding muscle. The shoulder seam must land exactly here for correct drape. In professional fitting speech 肩宽 (shoulder width as measurement) is more idiomatic; 肩膀 stays on the body side.

改小 → take in (fix 肥)收小（解决肥的问题）

袖子 xiùzi

Fabric tube from shoulder seam to cuff. In compound types (长袖, 短袖) the 子 drops — don't say 长袖子. Standalone 袖子 is correct in fit complaints.
⚠ Problem问题：袖子有点儿长 The sleeves are a bit long袖子有点长
✓ Fix改法 改短 → shorten改短
改长 → lengthen改长

腰 yāo

The waist zone — felt, not measured. Distinct from 腰围 (the number in cm). 腰有点肥 = the zone feels baggy; 腰围68厘米 = the measurement is 68cm.
⚠ Problem问题：裤子的腰那里有点儿肥 The trouser waist is a bit baggy (肥 = ill-fitting)裤子腰部有点肥（肥 = 不合身）
✓ Fix改法 肥 → 改小
紧 → 放大 / 改大

量尺寸
liàng chǐ cun
to take measurements
用尺子测量各部位尺寸
来，我给你量量尺寸。
Come, let me take your measurements.
Tone trap: 量 here = liàng ↘ (4th, to measure) ≠ liáng ↗ (2nd, quantity/amount as in 数量、质量). Opposite tones, opposite grammatical roles.
图片
改
gǎi
to alter (a garment)
修改衣服尺寸使其合身
没问题，这个也可以改小。
No problem, that can be taken in too.
Physical reshaping for fit — not fixing a mistake (改正) or repairing (修). Key result compounds: 改好, 改短, 改大, 改小. Object placement: 改裤子尺寸 ✓ / *改尺寸裤子 ✗.

要求
yāoqiú
requirements (n.) / to require (v.)
提出的标准或需求（名/动词）
都可以照我的要求改小或者放大。
All can be altered to my specifications.
Emotionally neutral — no confrontational charge unlike English "demand." In the passage: 照我的要求 = "according to my specifications." A tailor or manager 要求 things as a matter of course.

满意
mǎnyì
satisfied / pleased
对结果或状况感到满足
我对那个料子也很满意。
I was very satisfied with the fabric too.
Stative adjective, not transitive verb. Object of satisfaction needs 对-fronting: ✓ 我对那个料子很满意 / ✗ 我满意那个料子. Bare 满意 = short affirmative answer.

样子
yàngzi
appearance / the way something looks
外观；形态；事物呈现的方式
你看，照这个样子做，可以吗？
Look, can you make it following this design?
Unifies three domains English splits: visual form (房子的样子), demeanor (一副生气的样子), manner of action (他工作的样子). 照样子做 = make it following the appearance of the model.
图片
款式
kuǎn shì
design / style / cut (of a product)
产品的设计风格或型号
这件裙子的款式很显身材。
This dress's cut really flatters the figure.
Product-centric — a dress has a 款式, a person does not. Narrower than 样子. Presupposes factory-made goods with selectable variants: 你们有什么款式? = "What models do you carry?"

Source Text原文

原文

Key vocabulary underlined — hover for English gloss. Toggle pinyin above.关键词汇下划线标注——悬停查看英文注释。

来中国以后，我听很多人说在这里找裁缝做衣服又好又便宜，所以我也非常想试试。我上网搜索了一下儿，发现了一个很大的布料市场，市场里面有很多裁缝店。你可以带自己的衣服或者杂志去那里，先选料子，然后请裁缝照样子做。

对布料市场和裁缝店都了解好了以后，我和一个朋友一起去了那里。我带了一本杂志，打算请裁缝照杂志上的名牌西装做。照我朋友说的，我们选了一家比较大、生意比较好的裁缝店。老板娘看了杂志上的那套名牌西装以后，帮我选了最适合的料子。我对那个料子也很满意。然后老板娘给我量了尺寸，告诉我一个星期以后就可以去取。

一个星期以后，我一个人又去了那个布料市场。我的西装已经做好了。我试了试，质量非常好，和杂志上的名牌西装一样。不过我还是发现了一点儿小问题：肩膀那里有点儿紧，袖子有点儿长，裤子的腰那里有点儿肥。老板娘说这些都不是大问题，都可以照我的要求改小或者放大。我希望老板娘可以改得快一点儿。老板娘说她马上就帮我改，我第二天就可以取。

阅读理解

Having absorbed the domain, these should come easily — no looking up required.已吸收领域词汇后，这些问题应能自然作答，无需查阅。
  1. 他是怎么知道那个布料市场的？
    Tā shì zěnme zhīdao nà ge bùliào shìchǎng de?
    What did he do online, and what verb describes that action?
  2. 他在那里做了什么，是照什么做的？
    Tā zài nàlǐ zuò le shénme, shì zhào shénme zuò de?
    What object did he bring, and what did it represent for the tailor? Think: 照样子做.
  3. 在那里做一套西装大概要多长时间？做好一套西装他一共要去几次？
    Zài nàlǐ zuò yī tào xīzhuāng dàgài yào duō cháng shíjiān?
    Two sub-questions. Read the timeline carefully — count every trip to the 布料市场.
  4. 第一次去取西装的时候，他发现了什么问题？老板娘打算帮他做什么？
    Dì-yī cì qù qǔ xīzhuāng de shíhou, tā fāxiàn le shénme wèntí?
    Three fit zones affected — name them. What is the word for what the 老板娘 will do to the garment?
